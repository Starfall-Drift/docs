
# What is Starfall Drift?

**Starfall Drift** is a fork of Funky that I'm messing with as a personal pet project. Basically just my (TrixxedHeart) own take on what I want out of SS14.

It's probably never gonna be its own server, at least not one *I* host, because god, I do not want to deal with that nightmare. I am also not currently interested in having someone host anything for me, at least not yet until I have this at a stage that I find "ready."

At most, any servers using this that I host personally will likely be playtests, not any sort of full server.

## The Vision
This is purely for me. I'm building everything **I** personally want to see in Space Station, following **my own directive vision** from top to bottom. No compromise. No collaboration stress. No twisting my ideas to fit into someone else's vision. This exists because I wanted a space where my ideas don't have to bend for anyone else.
## Lore & Thematics > Gameplay
Starfall Drift puts **lore and thematics first**. Gameplay exists to serve the story, not the other way around.
- **Antagonists exist, but they're not the main event.** Rounds aren't built around waiting for a traitor to do something. Conflict happens organically and everything is structured around to encourage it.
- **Anyone can be an "antagonist" within sensible reason.** Tensions, personal grudges, bad calls, and messy situations can turn a normal crewmember into a problem without needing the game to text prompt telling them to be one.
## Lore is Not Required Reading
I'm going to write lore because *I* enjoy it and it helps set the tone. But here's the important part:
**Nobody is ever going to get in trouble for "not following lore."**
Not knowing something? Fine. Ignoring it entirely? Fine. Breaking lore by accident or on purpose? Also fine. The lore exists to enrich the experience for people who want it, not to be a rulebook that gets swung at anyone who didn't do their homework.

At the end of the day, Starfall Drift is designed to be one big improv experience. Everyone is here to stretch their creative writing muscles and see what happens when you throw a bunch of characters into a drifting tin can in space.
The existing lore is just **groundwork**. A starting point. A vibe to bounce off of.
Go wild. Take the lore and run in a completely different direction. Do your own thing. Mix it with something else. The only goal is to create something interesting together, not to stay "canon compliant."
## Lore Beyond the Station
The world outside the hull matters. There will be more elaboration on history and places you'll never visit but will *feel* the consequences of. The station is a stage. The lore is the play. But you don't have to read the script to be in the play.
