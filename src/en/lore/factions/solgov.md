# SolGov
SolGov is a vast interstellar federal republic and economic union comprising hundreds of
member species and planetary governments. Modeled after historic supranational unions,
SolGov functions as a superstructure of standardized trade law, mutual defense, and
bureaucratic governance that often supersedes local planetary authority.

While its name references humanity's sun for historical reasons, SolGov is a truly multi-
species entity. Human influence is largely a historical footnote. Power is distributed among
the Trinity Systems, the three capital worlds that form the political, economic, and cultural
heart of the Union.

# History
SolGov emerged in the wake of humanity's early interstellar expansion, initially as a trade
and mutual defense pact between Earth and its first colonies. As contact was made with
other spacefaring species, the framework proved adaptable. Through a combination of
diplomatic offers, economic incentive, and coercion, it expanded to incorporate the
governments of dozens of species.

The SolGov Charter was signed in 2421, formalizing the transition from a humannocentric
alliance to a multi-species federal entity. The decision to move the administrative capital
from Earth to the newly constructed Citadel in the Epsilon Prime system symbolized this
shift. Earth, ecologically and economically depleted, was designated a Heritage World, its
fate a permanent reminder of the perils of mismanagement.
# Politics
## Governance
SolGov operates on two primary levels:

### Member Governments
Each member planet, species-nation, or orbital polity retains sovereignty over local affairs,
culture, and internal law. These can range from Unathi clan-holds and Vox trade-arks to
Lumari hive-colonies and human republics.
### The SolGov Superstructure
This layer governs all interstellar matters:
- Interstellar Trade & Commerce Regulation
- Currency Standardization & Central Banking
- Issuance of Corporate Charters
- The SolGov Fleet (collective defense and peacekeeping)
- The Civil Service & Bureaucracy (administration of Concord-wide law)

The highest political body is the **Concord Assembly**, a massive legislative chamber where
representatives from every member state debate and ratify Concord-wide law. Day-to-
day executive authority lies with the **Administrative Counci**l, a rotating body of officials
elected from the Assembly.
# Corporate Charters
A Corporate Charter in the SolGov Hegemony is a legal license issued by the Administrative Council that grants a megacorporation quasi-sovereign status and extraordinary rights of self-governance within specified jurisdictions. Chartered corporations function as non-territorial member entities of the Hegemony, wielding political and economic influence comparable to and in some cases exceeding that of planetary member states.

The charter system is a cornerstone of SolGov's political economy, enabling rapid interstellar development and technological monopolies that create tensions between corporate, state, and sovereignty.

## Legal and granting process eligibility criteria
To be considered for a charter, a corporation must demonstrate:
- Economic or technological indispensability to interstellar civilization (e.g. control of critical infrastructure, proprietary FTL technology, or unique biotech).
- Financial and organizational stability over a minimum of five SolGov decades (50 years).
- A history of compliance with SolGov regulations and interstellar law.
- The capacity for self-governance in isolated or hazardous environments, such as deepspace stations or frontier colonies.
